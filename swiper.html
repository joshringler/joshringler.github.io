CP Nassau's
Festival of Trees

By Kim Dossett and Joshua Ringler

Cerebral Palsy Nassau’s 28th Annual Festival of Trees took place from November 24th to 26th at the Cradle of Aviation in Garden City.

The event included live performances such as the Uniondale High School Band and various dance groups.

The event included live performances and vendors selling anything from hair accessories to holiday decorations to fresh honey.

While vendors are able to keep the profit from their sales, they pay a $450 fee that goes directly to CP Nassau.

Vendors included Department 56 as well as local businesses such as Miss Molly’s Honeydrippers.

“I’ve been doing Miss Molly’s Honeydrippers part-time for about four years, but this is the largest show I’ve done. It’s great to see everyone come out for a good cause.” -Molly, Vendor

Aside from the vendors and entertainment, the main event were the fully decorated trees.

Various organizations and companies, such as the Syosset High School National Art Honor Society, purchased a tree from CP Nassau and fully decorated them on Tuesday, November 24th to be auctioned off.

All ornaments, the tree skirt, and tree topper are donated by the organization or business decorating the tree.

Each tree is bid on, but depending on the value of the decorations on the tree some trees may start at higher bidding prices.

“We bought 38 trees this year, but we usually buy about 40. A lot of decorators come back the same every year.” - Lois, CP Nassau

And one lucky person went home with this tree that had over $2000 of concert tickets!

Although there is a $15 entrance fee that benefits CP Nassau as well as the Cradle of Aviation, all proceeds from the tree auctions benefit Cerebral Palsy Nassau.
